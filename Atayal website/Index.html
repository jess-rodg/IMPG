Atayal

As the most widespread ethnic group in Taiwan, the Atayal are distributed in the mountainous areas in central and northern Taiwan. According to the Atayal legend, the Atayal originated from a rock cracking where their ancestors (a male and a female) were born. Then, they migrated to the places where different tribes settle. Currently, the Atayal population is about 92,084 people, and this website aims to educate people on the livelihood of the Atayal people in order to protect their culture.

Importance of preserving indigenous cultures

Indigenous peoples hold a rich diversity of living heritage, including practices, representations, expressions, knowledge and skills that continue to be relevant and provide meaning in everyday life. The practice and transmission of this heritage contributes to the ongoing vitality, strength and wellbeing of communities.
